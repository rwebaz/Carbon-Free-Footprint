CFL Grow Guide


How to get started growing with CFLs

Cannabuds Grow Store

The first step in our tutorial on how to grow Med Mj using 'Compact Fluorescent Lights' (CFL) is to invest in and develop your choice of initial genetics ($100).

Next, you will need to secure and maintain a primary set of 'mother' plants from your genetics in a separate vegetative room.

Thirdly, you must then learn how to subsequently extract clones from your primary 'mother' plants in order to vegetatively develop viable, 'non-hermie' type female candidates for eventual introduction into a separate flowering-budding room.

Choosing Your Growing Medium

Using a 'Coco coir' ($15) y 'perlite' ($36) mix of indoor soil manufactured by Aurora Innovations of Eugene, Oregon USA, fill a (3) gallon grow bag ($5) w 1" to 2" inches of 'perlite', topped off by a 50/50 mix of your new 'Coco coir y perlite' indoor soil mix reserve.

Next, insert a 5" female clone into your now indoor 'pro' soil mix stuff'd (3) gallon grow bag ($5).

Set Up Your Light Grid

Using up to twelve, 42 watt CFL 'full spectrum' light bulbs ($17) and up to twelve corresponding 6 ft. length, medium bulb socket connectors with deflector shields ($6), set up and suspend a 500 watt grid or array approx. 5" above your 5" clone(s).

As a 'Rule of Thumb' for properly spacing the bulbs within your grid or array, you should be able to fit one 42 watt CFL bulb over each 3-5 gall container placed under your grid or array system within your vegetative room.

For example (por ejemplo), space your lamps in a 5 x 5 (25 sq ft.) array as shown in the subject picture and then suspend the lighting system over your plants.

It's that simple!

Now, as you raise the vertical distance of your suspended grid or array above your plants, the luminosity of your CFL grid or array will become dispersed inversely proportional to the distance from above thus diminishing the 'brightness' of your display.

In other words, as your plants grow and as you increase the vertical height of your CFL grid or array, the light that penetrates and reaches into the lower part of your garden's canopy will become weakened.

Therefore, be sure to increase the distance between the tops of your plant(s) and your new suspended CFL bulb grid or array to decrease any heat impact that may be generated by your new lighting system and consider adding additional side lighting to compensate for the eventual inverse squaring of your light source distance.

Next, go get a 'mechanical' timer ($10) and two safety power strips ($15) to plug your new CFL grid or array into, and then schedule your plants for (18) hrs of light on...(8) hours of light off.

Time to Nourish

Start feeding your 5" clone(s) a dissolved in water solution based on a recommended feeding schedule for vegetative stage plants ie.) 'Fox Farms' hydro chemical solutions ($28).

When the top layer of 'Coco coir' for each 3- 5 grow bag becomes dry, give your plants another splash of your calibrated nutrient solution.

Or, alternatively invest in a hydroponic feeding system that can deliver your calibrated nutrient solution via timed drip spikes, three times per day.

Remember Only use 'reverse osmosis' water ($0.05 per gall) for your feeding system as the 'total dissolved salts' (tds) have been stripped from this breed of water.

In calibration parlance, the 'parts per million' (ppm)'s of 'total dissolved salts' (tds) in reverse osmosis water are 'zero'.

From 'zero', you can then calibrate your plant's nutrient absorption rate based on the level of (tds) recommended by your chemical solution vendor's 'professional feeding schedule' along with a reading of the current (pH) or 'negative decimal logarithm of the hydrogen ion activity' of both your nutrient solution reservoir and your indoor soil mix.

Ultimately, to check both the level of (tds) and the level of (pH) in your 'nutrient solution + water' reservoir, and in your 3-5 gall grow bags, you will need to purchase a detection 'meter' that can automatically sense in real time both the (pH) level and the (tds) level of your chemical feeding solution and your grow bags. As a bonus, the detection meter should also be able to optionally reveal the current temperature of your chemical feeding solution ($136).

Despite the cost of your 'ultimate' meter, you will still need to check and calibrate the meter weekly, if not daily, to insure that the expensive little 'sucker' is still working properly!

Otherwise, you may 'burn' your plant(s) by feeding them TOO MUCH nutrient solution n water (too many tds's), or conversely you may starve your plants by feeding them NOT ENOUGH nutrient solution n water (too little tds's).

"It's a chemical balancing act between you and your plant(s) throughout the entire vegetative growth cycle from seed germination, to cloning extraction, to mother plant husbandry all the way into the budding cycle and straight into the harvest or 'curing' period." -Robert Hempaz, PhD. Trichometry™

So, be diligent and don't 'scrimp' when it comes to purchasing your garden meter.

You will have a wide range of possibilities to choose from when it is time to pull the trigger on purchasing your garden meter.

And, don't forget the temperature reading of your nutrient solution n water reservoir!

Too cold (< 68 deg F.), and you will 'shock' your plant's root system.

Too warm, (> 90 deg F.) and the roots of your plant will become stressed and not be able to absorb all of those fancy chemicals and micro-nutrients you have been feeding your plant(s) in the first place!

Oh, and also please check to see what is the optimum (pH) level range as set by your nutrient solution chemical vendor.

This is the range where depending on the medium the plant(s) are actually being grown in, whereby the roots of the plants are best tuned in to absorb the myriad of micro-nutrients and chemicals included in your nutrient solution chemical vendor's product.

Usually, with 'indoor soil' types of medium like 'Coco coir y perlite', the range of (pH) calibration extends from the slightly acidic 5.5 reading to the moderatly akaline (or, 'base') reading of 6.1

Try a (pH) reading of 5.8 to start.

Yield

With respect to future 'yield' estimates...Figure your plant(s) under this simple CFL growing system will produce initially about a quarter pound (112 gr) per plant of chronic grade, fully crypt'd Med Mj buds over a ninety to one-hundred and twenty day vegetative and budding period cycle.

And, these estimates do NOT count the volume of shake, trimmings and clippings you will take over the course of the growing period.

Even enough to make a supplemental batch of (CBD) enriched 'bubble hash' as an aside!

Now, that comes to a savings of over $1,900 based on the ongoing market rate (California) of $17 per gram for good quality medicinal cannabis buds!

Finally, do watch both the ambient air temperature and the humidity readings in both of your grow rooms. For this reading, you will need a combo thermometer-hygrometer ($12).

Keep the air temp of your budding room below 90 degrees at all times, and only reduce the natural humidity (40% - 50%) of the budding room during the final two weeks of the flowering stage.

It is important to keep the relative humidity of your budding room 'dry' at around 40% - 50% throughout the entire flowering cycle. This level of humidity will also most likely NOT induce an environment conducive to the growth of plant pests and plant fungi, molds, etc.

However, if your garden does get caught with an infestation of 'Tetranychus urticae' (red spider mites), for example, try spraying on a soaking of natural 'cinnamaldehyde' to impede the little critters without damaging your crop.

So, look forward to the day that you will be able to stuff your 'nug jars' full of home-grown medicine, my dear physican referred patients, and we'll keep the 'Lights-O-Information' glowing for ya!
